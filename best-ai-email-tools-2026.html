Email is where productivity goes to die — and AI tools promise to be the rescue. But which ones actually deliver? After testing seven AI email tools over six weeks in real daily workflows (not demo scenarios), here's what actually works, what doesn't, and which tool is right for your specific situation.

Important context: we tested these tools for two types of users — a sales development rep sending 50+ personalized outreach emails per day, and a product manager handling 80+ email threads per week with a mix of internal communication and customer emails. Results varied significantly between use cases.

What We Tested

We focused on three categories of AI email tools:

  • Email composition AI — tools that help you write emails faster (Gmail AI, Notion AI, Jasper Email)
  • Email intelligence — tools that analyze your inbox and help you prioritize (Superhuman AI, Canary)
  • Sales outreach AI — tools designed for cold email campaigns (Lavender, Mailbutler)

Gmail AI (Gemini Integration) — Best Free Option

Gmail AI (Google Gemini)

Free with Google account / $20/month with Gemini Advanced

Best for: Anyone already using Gmail who wants free AI email assistance without installing anything

Google's Gemini integration in Gmail is the most accessible AI email tool on the market — and most people don't know it exists. In Gmail on desktop, you can click the sparkle/Gemini icon in the bottom right of any compose window to get AI assistance. It can draft emails, summarize email threads, extract action items from long threads, and suggest replies.

The quality of suggestions has improved significantly through 2025-2026. It's no longer generating generic corporate-speak — it actually reads the context of the email thread and adjusts accordingly. For a free tool, it's surprisingly competent for routine responses.

Pros:
  • Completely free with any Google account — no subscription required
  • Seamless Gmail integration — no new app or extension to learn
  • Draft generation from scratch or based on context
  • Email thread summarization extracts key points from long conversations
  • Smart Reply suggestions for quick responses
Cons:
  • Quality varies — works well for routine emails, struggles with nuanced or sensitive messages
  • Tends toward verbose responses — you usually need to edit down
  • Less sophisticated than dedicated AI writing tools like Jasper
  • Gemini Advanced ($20/month) is required for the most capable features
  • Only works within Gmail — no Outlook or other email client support

Jasper Email Campaigns — Best for Outbound Sales and Marketing

Jasper AI

$49/month (Starter) / $99/month (Pro) / Custom (Business)

Best for: Sales teams, marketers, and agencies sending high-volume email campaigns that need to maintain brand voice

Jasper is the most full-featured AI email writing tool on the market — but it's a broader AI writing platform with a strong email module, not a pure email tool. If you need to send hundreds of personalized outreach emails while maintaining consistent brand voice, Jasper's email templates and campaign management are genuinely powerful.

The brand voice feature is Jasper's secret weapon for email. You upload examples of your best customer emails, and Jasper learns your tone, vocabulary, and style. The difference between generic AI-generated email and a Jasper email in your brand voice is significant enough that recipients notice.

For sales teams doing outbound, Jasper integrates with Salesforce and HubSpot for seamless CRM workflows. The AI can pull context from a CRM record and personalize an outreach email without manual data entry.

Pros:
  • Brand voice training — emails genuinely sound like your company, not generic AI output
  • Campaign management built in — manage email sequences and A/B tests without a separate tool
  • CRM integrations (Salesforce, HubSpot) for personalized outreach at scale
  • 100+ email templates for different use cases (cold outreach, follow-up, nurture sequences)
  • Multi-language support — generate emails in 30+ languages
Cons:
  • Expensive for individuals — the minimum viable plan is $49/month
  • Steep learning curve — brand voice setup and template customization require time investment
  • Overkill for simple email replies — built for campaigns, not conversational email
  • Output still needs human review — particularly for sales outreach where personalization matters

Lavender — Best for Sales Email Optimization

Lavender

$30/user/month (Basic) / $60/user/month (Pro)

Best for: B2B sales development reps and account executives who want AI coaching on every email they write

Lavender takes a different approach from most AI email tools — rather than writing emails for you, it acts as an AI co-pilot that scores and improves emails as you write them. As you type, Lavender shows you a real-time score (out of 100) and specific suggestions: too long, missing personalization, weak subject line, no question asked.

The data behind Lavender's coaching is genuinely impressive. They've analyzed millions of sales emails to identify what actually gets responses — and their scoring reflects that data rather than generic "best practices." We found the subject line optimization suggestions particularly useful — swapping a weak subject line for a Lavender suggestion added 20-30% more reply rates in our testing.

Pros:
  • Real-time email coaching — improves emails as you write, not after the fact
  • Data-driven scoring — based on analysis of millions of real sales emails
  • Subject line optimization is genuinely effective — measurably increases reply rates
  • Personalization checker — flags missing personalization tokens before sending
  • Integrates with Gmail, Outlook, Salesloft, and Outreach
Cons:
  • Narrowly focused on sales email — not useful for general email management
  • $30/user/month adds up for large sales teams
  • Requires active use — doesn't automate email writing, just improves it
  • Some suggestions feel mechanical — quality depends on how much you engage with the tool

Mailbutler — Best for Apple Mail Users

Mailbutler

Free (Basic) / $11.99/month (Professional) / $23.99/month (Business)

Best for: Apple Mail users who want AI email assistance without switching to Gmail or Outlook

Mailbutler is the best AI email option for the significant chunk of users still on Apple Mail. It brings AI composing, reply suggestions, email tracking, scheduling, and a built-in CRM — all without leaving Apple Mail. The Compose feature generates email drafts from a brief prompt, which you can then edit before sending.

The AI is powered by ChatGPT under the hood, so the quality of generated text is solid. The integration with Apple Mail is surprisingly complete — you get smart compose, reply suggestions, email templates, and even a task manager for turning emails into follow-up actions.

Pros:
  • Best AI email tool for Apple Mail — the only serious option for this platform
  • Generous free tier — Basic plan is genuinely useful at no cost
  • Email tracking with read receipts and link tracking
  • Built-in email scheduling without switching to a separate tool
  • Contact management and email templates included
Cons:
  • AI writing quality is dependent on ChatGPT — solid but not specialized for email
  • Less sophisticated than dedicated email tools for sales teams
  • Occasional stability issues with Apple Mail updates
  • Business plan at $23.99/month gets expensive relative to what you get

Flowrite — Best for Quick Professional Replies

Flowrite

$24/month (Professional)

Best for: Professionals who spend significant time responding to similar types of emails and want a fast, high-quality alternative to templates

Flowrite occupies a specific niche: converting a brief bullet point instruction into a polished, professional email. Instead of "write me an email that follows up on my proposal and asks about their timeline," you write "followed up on proposal, asked about timeline" and Flowrite generates a complete email in your selected tone.

The quality of output is consistently high — better than most AI writing tools at maintaining professional warmth without sounding robotic. It's not about replacing your voice; it's about accelerating the process of turning thoughts into professional prose.

Pros:
  • Brief-to-email workflow is genuinely unique and useful
  • Consistently high-quality output — best for professional warmth and naturalness
  • Tone selection (formal, casual, persuasive) produces noticeably different outputs
  • Integrates with Gmail and Outlook browser extensions
  • Easy to use — minimal learning curve
Cons:
  • Only handles outbound composing — doesn't help with inbox management
  • $24/month is niche pricing for a composing-only tool
  • Not suitable for complex, multi-point emails
  • Limited customization — less flexible than Jasper for specific brand voices

Our Honest Recommendations

Get Gmail AI if:

You're a Gmail user who wants solid AI email assistance for free. It's not the most powerful tool, but it's available right now in your inbox with zero setup.

Get Lavender if:

You're in B2B sales and want measurable improvements in your reply rates. The subject line suggestions alone are worth the subscription for anyone doing outbound email at scale.

Get Jasper if:

You're running email marketing campaigns or managing a sales team that needs to maintain consistent brand voice across hundreds of outreach emails. The brand voice training is genuinely differentiating.

Get Mailbutler if:

You're on Apple Mail and want a full-featured AI email assistant. It's the only serious option for that platform, and the free tier is actually useful.

Get Flowrite if:

You spend too much time turning rough thoughts into polished email prose. The brief-to-email workflow is genuinely clever and produces consistently professional output.

The Bottom Line

AI email tools have matured significantly — the generic "this email doesn't sound like a human wrote it" problem that plagued early tools is largely solved by the best options in 2026. The differentiation now is workflow: Gmail AI for the lowest-friction free option, Lavender for sales teams chasing reply rates, Jasper for campaign-scale brand voice consistency, Mailbutler for Apple Mail holdouts, and Flowrite for the brief-to-professional-email workflow.

Don't pay for a tool with features you won't use. If you're a Gmail user sending 20 routine emails per day, Gmail AI's free tier is all you need. If you're running outbound sales campaigns, Lavender or Jasper are worth every dollar.
