Best AI Meeting Tools 2026

Otter.ai vs Fireflies vs Trint vs Fathom vs Fellow — We Tested Them All in Real Team Meetings

四月 7, 2026 · 11 min read · By Morai

Meeting notes are a necessary evil in most organizations — someone has to take them, everyone hates doing it, and even when they're taken well, they're usually incomplete, biased by the note-taker's perspective, and filed away never to be read again. AI meeting tools promise to fix this by automatically transcribing, summarizing, and action-extracting from your meetings. We tested five of the leading options across 30+ real meetings over six weeks to find out which actually delivers.

What We Tested

We ran each tool through the same gauntlet: weekly team standups (fast-paced, multiple speakers), client discovery calls (technical jargon, accents), board presentations (single speaker, Q&A format), and brainstorming sessions (overlapping speech, creative language). We evaluated transcription accuracy, speaker identification, summary quality, action item extraction, and integration with our existing workflow tools.

Otter.ai — The Established Standard

Otter.ai

Free (300 mins/month) / $10/month (Pro) / $20/user/month (Business)

Best for: Teams already using Zoom or Google Meet who want automatic transcription without a learning curve

Otter.ai has been the reference standard for AI meeting transcription since 2019, and it shows. After years of refinement, it's the most reliable and easiest to use of all the tools we tested — join a meeting, let it run, and come back to accurate transcripts, summaries, and action items. The interface is clean, the export options are comprehensive, and the integrations with Zoom, Google Meet, and Microsoft Teams are seamless.

The AI Chat feature is genuinely useful — you can ask Otter questions about your meeting ("What were the three main concerns raised?") and get natural language answers from the transcript. This transforms the transcript from a reference document into an interactive knowledge base. For teams that have accumulated hundreds of hours of recorded meetings, being able to query that knowledge is genuinely powerful.

Pros:
  • Most reliable and consistent transcription accuracy of all tools tested
  • AI Chat for querying transcripts — turn meeting archives into a searchable knowledge base
  • Automatic speaker identification is accurate even in small groups (4-8 people)
  • Seamless Zoom, Google Meet, and Teams integration
  • Automatic summary generation after each meeting — no manual post-processing
Cons:
  • Free tier (300 mins/month) is barely enough for light personal use — team adoption requires paid plans
  • Summary quality is solid but sometimes misses nuance in fast-moving discussions
  • Action item extraction requires calibration — sometimes picks up non-action items
  • Interface can be slow with very long transcripts (20+ meetings accumulated)

Fireflies.ai — Best for Sales and Revenue Teams

Fireflies.ai

Free (800 mins/month) / $10/user/month (Pro) / $19/user/month (Business)

Best for: Sales teams, recruiters, and customer success managers who need CRM integration and conversation intelligence

Fireflies.ai takes the meeting transcription base and builds a full conversation intelligence platform on top of it — and that platform is genuinely impressive for the right use cases. The CRM integration (Salesforce, HubSpot, Slack) means call summaries and action items flow automatically into your existing sales stack. For a sales team, this eliminates the manual data entry that makes CRM updates feel like punishment.

The conversation analytics — tracking talk-to-listen ratios, sentiment analysis, and topic tracking across calls — are genuinely useful for sales managers trying to coach their teams. You can identify which reps talk too much (and listen too little), which calls generate the most positive sentiment, and which topics most correlate with closed deals.

Pros:
  • Strongest CRM integrations — automatic call logging to Salesforce and HubSpot is a game-changer for sales
  • Conversation intelligence — talk ratios, sentiment, and topic tracking across calls
  • Generous free tier (800 mins/month) — actually viable for individual use
  • AI summaries are highly structured and consistent
  • Collaboration features — comment, react, and share clips from transcripts
Cons:
  • Transcription accuracy slightly trails Otter in our testing, particularly with accents
  • Feature set is sales/Customer Success focused — less relevant for general team meetings
  • Interface has more features than casual users need — can feel overwhelming
  • AI-generated follow-up emails sometimes need significant editing

Trint — Best for Journalists and Content Creators

Trint

$24/month (Individual) / Custom (Teams)

Best for: Journalists, podcasters, documentary filmmakers, and anyone who transcribes and then edits interview footage

Trint takes a different approach from the meeting-focused tools — it's designed for professional transcription workflows where the transcript is a first-class artifact, not just meeting notes. The timeline view that synchronizes transcript text with audio/video playback is genuinely useful for video editors and podcast producers who need to find specific moments in hours of footage.

Transcription quality for interview and documentary content is excellent — Trint's speech recognition was trained specifically on broadcast-quality audio, which gives it an edge for professional recording setups. For Zoom calls with variable audio quality, Otter still has the edge.

Pros:
  • Synchronized transcript + audio/video timeline — unique feature for video editing
  • Multilingual transcription in 50+ languages — best for international content
  • Export formats are designed for professional workflows (SRT, VTT, XML for Premiere)
  • Collaboration features built for editorial teams — assign sections, add editorial notes
  • Mobile app for recording and transcribing on the go
Cons:
  • Pricing ($24/month) is high for individual users who don't need professional video sync
  • Meeting-specific features (action items, calendar integration) less developed than competitors
  • Interface is more complex than Otter or Fireflies — not as immediately intuitive
  • Free tier is very limited (3 hours) — not enough for regular testing

Fathom — Best Free Option

Fathom

Free (Personal) / $10/user/month (Teams)

Best for: Budget-conscious teams who want meeting transcription without per-minute costs, and individuals who just need basic call recording and transcription

Fathom's headline is simple: unlimited meeting transcription for free. That alone makes it worth considering — the other free tiers in this category cap you at 300-800 minutes per month, which means heavy meeting schedules burn through the free allocation quickly. Fathom's unlimited approach is refreshingly straightforward.

The quality of transcription is competitive with Otter and Fireflies for standard Zoom and Google Meet calls. Where Fathom trails is in the intelligence layer — the summarization and action item features are less sophisticated, and the CRM integrations are more limited. For basic transcription + summary needs, Fathom is an excellent free option.

Pros:
  • Unlimited meeting transcription on free plan — genuinely unique in this category
  • Quick setup — one-click install for Zoom and Google Meet
  • Automatic highlight reel generation — captures key moments as you go
  • Keyboard shortcuts for real-time note-taking during meetings
  • $10/user/month Teams plan is competitive with Otter and Fireflies
Cons:
  • AI summarization less sophisticated than Otter or Fireflies
  • CRM integrations more limited — not the sales intelligence platform Fireflies is
  • Brand new company (2023) — less track record than established competitors
  • Action item extraction less accurate in fast-moving discussions

Our Honest Recommendations

Get Otter.ai if:

You're a general team that needs reliable, accurate meeting transcription with good summaries and collaborative features. The AI Chat feature for querying past transcripts is genuinely differentiating for teams that accumulate large meeting archives.

Get Fireflies.ai if:

You're in sales, recruiting, or customer success and need automatic CRM logging, conversation intelligence, and coaching analytics. The Salesforce/HubSpot integration alone can save your team hours of manual data entry per week.

Get Trint if:

You're a journalist, podcaster, or video producer who works with interview footage and needs synchronized transcript + media editing. The multilingual transcription is also the best in class for international content.

Get Fathom if:

You want unlimited meeting transcription for free and don't need sophisticated CRM integrations or conversation intelligence. For individuals and small teams with basic needs, it's the best budget option.

The Bottom Line

After 30+ real meetings across all tools: Otter.ai is the most reliable all-rounder, Fireflies.ai is the best investment for sales and revenue teams, Trint is the professional choice for broadcast and documentary content, and Fathom is the best free option for basic transcription needs.

The days of designated note-takers are numbered. For most teams, the time saved on meeting documentation alone justifies the subscription cost of any of these tools — and that's before you factor in the searchable meeting archive that comes from running these tools consistently.
