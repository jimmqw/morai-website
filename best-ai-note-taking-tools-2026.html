Best AI Note-Taking Tools 2026

Notion AI vs Obsidian vs RemNote vs Bear AI — We Tested Them for Knowledge Management and Daily Use

四月 7, 2026 · 13 min read · By Morai

Notes are only useful if you can find them again — and most people can't. The average knowledge worker creates hundreds of notes per year and retrieves almost none of them, because the tools they use don't help them connect ideas across time. AI note-taking tools promise to fix this by automatically organizing, linking, and surfacing information when you need it. After testing five AI note-taking tools across eight weeks in real knowledge work — research, project management, daily notes, and long-term knowledge building — here's what actually works in 2026.

What We Tested

We used each tool for the same knowledge work: maintaining a daily note habit, building a personal knowledge base from articles and research, managing project documentation, and trying to find information from notes created weeks or months earlier. We specifically evaluated how AI features help — or don't help — with the retrieval problem that makes most note-taking systems fail.

Notion AI — Best All-in-One Workspace with AI

Notion AI

$10/user/month (add-on to $10/user/month Notion Plus)

Best for: Teams and individuals who want a complete workspace for notes, docs, wikis, and project management with AI assistance built in

Notion AI is not a note-taking app with AI added — it's an AI layer on top of a complete knowledge management platform. That distinction matters. Notion's strength has always been the database and block-based structure that lets you organize information in interconnected pages, tables, and wikis. Notion AI adds AI capabilities directly into that structure: ask questions about your workspace, auto-generate content, summarize pages, and extract action items from meeting notes.

The AI Q&A feature is genuinely useful for navigating large knowledge bases. Ask "what was our decision about the pricing strategy?" and it searches across your entire Notion workspace and returns an answer — no need to remember which page the decision was on. For teams with years of accumulated Notion pages, this is transformative.

Pros:
  • AI Q&A across entire workspace — genuinely solves the retrieval problem for accumulated knowledge
  • AI writing assistance integrated into the block editor — draft, summarize, improve directly within pages
  • Database structure enables powerful linking between notes, projects, and people
  • Team collaboration is best-in-class — shared workspaces, comments, permissions
  • Auto-generated meeting notes from audio (with integrations)
Cons:
  • Monthly cost is significant: $10 base + $10 AI = $20/user/month for full functionality
  • Learning curve is steep — Notion's flexibility can overwhelm new users
  • Not a native mobile-first experience — web app works, but mobile apps are less polished
  • Offline support requires setup and syncing can be finicky
  • Not ideal for purely linear note-taking — better for structured knowledge management

Obsidian with AI Plugins — Best for Personal Knowledge Management

Obsidian + Copilot/Memex/Local GPT

$25 lifetime (Personal) / Free + plugin costs

Best for: Knowledge workers and researchers who want maximum control over their note structure, linking, and AI integration

Obsidian is fundamentally different from the other tools in this comparison — it's a local-first markdown editor with a powerful linking system, and the AI capabilities come from community-built plugins rather than being built into the app. This architecture gives you extraordinary flexibility: choose your own AI provider (OpenAI, Anthropic, local models), customize exactly how AI interacts with your notes, and own your data completely.

The core Obsidian feature that makes it powerful is the graph view — every note you create can link to every other note, and Obsidian visualizes those connections. Over time, you build a personal knowledge graph that surfaces unexpected connections between ideas. Adding AI via plugins adds natural language querying, auto-linking, and AI-assisted writing directly into this graph.

Pros:
  • Complete data ownership — all notes stored locally as markdown files
  • Graph view surfaces unexpected connections between notes over time
  • Plugin ecosystem is enormous — customize AI behavior exactly to your workflow
  • One-time purchase ($25) for Personal license — no recurring subscription for core app
  • Truly offline — works without internet, syncs when connected
Cons:
  • Learning curve is steep — requires deliberate setup and plugin curation
  • AI features depend on third-party plugins — quality and stability vary
  • No native team collaboration features — not designed for teams
  • Sync across devices requires third-party setup (iCloud, Dropbox, or Obsidian Sync)
  • Can feel like building your own tool rather than using one

RemNote — Best for Learning and Spaced Repetition

RemNote

Free (limited) / $8/month (Pro) / $15/month (Ultra)

Best for: Students, researchers, and anyone building knowledge through reading and study who wants AI assistance combined with scientifically-proven spaced repetition

RemNote occupies a unique niche: it's the only AI note-taking tool that's also a spaced repetition flashcard system. If you're building knowledge through study — reading papers, books, or courses — RemNote lets you capture notes and automatically generates flashcards from them, then schedules review using spaced repetition algorithms (SM-2 or FSRS) to maximize long-term retention.

The AI feature that makes RemNote special is AI-assisted flashcard generation. Instead of manually creating flashcards from your notes, RemNote's AI analyzes your notes and suggests flashcards, portals (concept mappings), and knowledge trees. This dramatically reduces the friction of building a spaced repetition practice — which is why most people fail with traditional flashcard apps.

Pros:
  • Spaced repetition system built in — genuinely useful for long-term knowledge retention
  • AI flashcard generation from notes — reduces manual card creation to near zero
  • Document AI reads and summarizes PDFs, papers, and books directly
  • Knowledge tree visualization shows how concepts connect
  • $8/month Pro is competitively priced for students and researchers
Cons:
  • Spaced repetition is a commitment — requires regular review sessions to be effective
  • Learning curve for understanding how to use the system effectively
  • Not ideal for project management or meeting notes — pure knowledge building focus
  • Export options limited — your notes are somewhat trapped in the RemNote ecosystem

Bear AI — Best for Apple Users

Bear AI

Free (limited) / $2.49/month (Premium)

Best for: Apple users who want a beautiful, simple AI note-taking app without the complexity of Notion or Obsidian

Bear is the note-taking app Apple should have built — it's beautifully designed, native on iPhone, iPad, and Mac, and focuses on what most people actually want from a notes app: a place to write, organized with tags, accessible everywhere. Bear AI adds Shiny (Bear's AI feature) for summarization, rewriting, and generation directly within the writing interface.

The Apple Notes comparison is inevitable — and Bear wins on aesthetics, tag organization, and the writing experience. Bear AI's advantage is Shiny: you can select any text and ask Bear to improve it, shorten it, make it professional, or explain it — all without leaving the writing interface.

Pros:
  • Best writing experience of any notes app — native, fast, beautifully designed
  • Tag-based organization is intuitive and powerful for personal knowledge management
  • Shiny AI features work directly in the writing interface without switching modes
  • $2.49/month is the cheapest premium subscription of any AI notes app
  • Excellent Apple Pencil support for iPad handwriting
Cons:
  • No native Windows or Android apps — Apple ecosystem only
  • Not designed for teams — no collaboration features
  • No database structure — can't build the interconnected knowledge graphs of Notion or Obsidian
  • AI features (Shiny) are less sophisticated than Notion AI or ChatGPT-powered tools

Apple Notes AI — Best Free Option for Apple Users

Apple Notes with Apple Intelligence

Free (included with Apple devices)

Best for: Apple users who want basic AI features without installing anything new

Apple's Intelligence features in Notes (writing tools, summarization, smart replies) are built directly into the app you've been using for years. The advantage is zero friction — no new app to learn, no subscription to manage. The disadvantage is that these are basic AI features, not a purpose-built AI knowledge management system.

For casual note-takers who just want to summarize meeting notes, improve writing, and search across their notes, Apple Notes + Apple Intelligence is genuinely sufficient. For serious knowledge workers who want interconnected notes, backlinks, and AI-assisted knowledge building, it's insufficient.

Pros:
  • Zero additional cost — included with Apple devices
  • Zero learning curve — you're already using Notes
  • Writing Tools (proofread, rewrite, summarize) work across all Apple apps
  • Deep system integration with Calendar, Reminders, and Siri
  • Excellent for quick captures — voice notes, scanned documents, photos
Cons:
  • Limited organization — folders and tags, no backlinks or graph view
  • AI features are basic compared to dedicated AI notes apps
  • Not designed for knowledge building over time
  • Apple-only — no cross-platform support

Our Honest Recommendations

Get Notion AI if:

You're a team that needs shared workspaces, wikis, and project management combined with AI that can query your entire knowledge base. The workspace-level AI Q&A is genuinely differentiating for teams with accumulated knowledge.

Get Obsidian + plugins if:

You're serious about personal knowledge management and want maximum control over your note structure, linking, and AI behavior. The graph view becomes more valuable over time as your knowledge base grows.

Get RemNote if:

You're studying, doing academic research, or building knowledge through reading and need spaced repetition combined with AI assistance. The flashcard generation alone is worth the subscription for serious learners.

Get Bear AI if:

You're an Apple user who wants a beautiful writing experience with basic AI assistance at the cheapest possible price. The $2.49/month is barely noticeable and the writing app is genuinely lovely.

Use Apple Notes if:

You're an Apple user who just needs basic note-taking with some AI features. The friction of zero — you're already using it — makes it the right choice for casual use, even if more powerful options exist.

The Bottom Line

The best AI note-taking tool depends entirely on your goal. For teams: Notion AI is the clear winner — the workspace-level AI Q&A and collaboration are unmatched. For personal knowledge building: Obsidian (with plugins) or RemNote depending on whether you prioritize linking and graph views (Obsidian) or spaced repetition (RemNote). For casual Apple users: Bear AI or Apple Notes depending on whether you want a beautiful writing app (Bear) or zero-friction integration (Apple Notes).

Whatever you choose: the most important feature of any notes app is whether you'll actually use it consistently. A powerful tool you don't open is worse than a simple tool that fits your natural workflow.
